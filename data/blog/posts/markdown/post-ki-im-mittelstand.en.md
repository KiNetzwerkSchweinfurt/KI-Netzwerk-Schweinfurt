# Starting AI realistically

Many companies ask how to begin with AI.

## Common startup mistakes

Across manufacturing, sales and administration teams, we repeatedly see the same patterns: goals are too broad, data foundations are weak, and ownership is unclear.

Instead of beginning with a large AI transformation project, a focused use case is usually more effective. Define measurable value early, such as time savings, lower error rates or faster response times.

## A practical rollout flow

1. Describe the business problem precisely.
2. Assess available data quality.
3. Define a pilot with explicit success criteria.
4. Test jointly with business and IT.
5. Decide early: stop, adapt or scale.

## What often helps in practice

Many teams move faster when they start with a small core group: business owner, IT counterpart and one person with clear decision authority. This avoids long decision loops and keeps execution grounded.

It is equally important to set realistic expectations. A pilot does not need to be perfect - it must prove whether value, data and process fit together. That clarity saves time and budget later.

## Conclusion

AI works best in SMB contexts when it is tightly connected to real processes and not treated as an isolated IT topic.

Teams that start with measurable goals, discuss risks openly and gather early feedback from day-to-day operations create the strongest path to scale.
